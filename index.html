Frequently asked questions

What is Certified Financial Planning (CFP)?

CFP is the highest international qualification in the field of personal finance globally.

Who affiliates CFP?

CFP is globally affiliated by Financial Planning Standards Board, Denver, US.

Where is CFP Recognised?

It is recognised in 27 countries (including India) all over the world namely in US, Europe, Australia, New Zealand, Switzerland, Germany, Singapore and other major global financial hubs.

Who is eligible to do CFP?

CFP can be done by any individual who has completed HSC or Diploma in any stream. Thus, any student from Science, Commerce and Arts background can do CFP.

What is the duration of CFP Course?

The entire can be completed within 15 months if pursued through online lectures and if pursued through recorded lectures then in 10 months.

Who recognises CFP in India?

CFP qualification is recognised by Securities Exchange Board of India (SEBI) for working in the field of Finance.

How are exams conducted in India?

In India, exams of CFP are conducted by National Stock Exchange through National Institute of Securities Market (NISM). Exams can be appeared both online as well as offline from various centres of NSE.

What is the exam pattern?

The exams are in MCQ format with NO negative marking. Exams are conducted in the third week of every month. The certificate of CFP is issued directly from FPSB, US.

Who hires CFP in India?

CFP qualified people are hired by all the companies incorporated in the Banking, Financial Services and Investment (BFSI) sector namely, Financial Planning firms, Mutual Fund AMC’s, Insurance Companies, Stock broking, Hedge Funds, Wealth Management and PMS firms in India.

What are the job profiles for a CFP qualified person?

A person qualified as a CFP can form their own Financial Planning firm or they can be employed in the capacity of Investment advisor, investment research and analyst, wealth manager or portfolio manager, finance content creator etc.
